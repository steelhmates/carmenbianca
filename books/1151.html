• AND JESUS passing by, saw a man blind: from his nativity; and his Disciples asked him: Rabbi, who hath sinned, this man, or his parents, that he should be born blind? JESUS answered: Neither hath this man sinned, nor his parents; but that the works of God may be manifested in him. I must work the works of him that sent me, whiles it is day. The night cometh, when no man can work. As long as I am in the world, I am the light of the world. When he had said these things, he spit on the ground, and made clay of the spittle, and spread the clay upon his eyes, and said to him: Go, wash in the pool of Siloe, which is interpreted, Sent. He went therefore, and washed; and he came seeing. Therefore the neighbours, and they which had seen him before, that he was a beggar, said:
