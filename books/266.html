• And when David & his men were come to Siceleg the third day, the Amalecites had made an invasion on the south side into Siceleg, & had strucken Siceleg, & burnt it with fire. And had led away women captive out of it, from the less unto the great: and had not killed any man, but had led them with them, & went on their journey. When David therefore and his men were come to the city, and had found it burnt with fire, and their wives and their sons, and their daughters to be led away captive, David and the people that was with him, lifted up their voices, and mourned till tears failed them. For the two wives also of David were led away captive, Achinoam the Iezraelite, and Abigail the wife of Nabal of Carmel. And David was strucken very sad: for the people would have stoned him, because the soul of every man was bitterly affected upon their sons, and daughters: but David was strengthened in our Lord his God.
