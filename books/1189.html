• BUT Agrippa said to Paul: Thou art permitted to speak for thyself. Then Paul stretching forth his hand, began to make his answer: Touching all things whereof I am accused of the Jews, king Agrippa, I account myself happy for that I am to defend myself this day before thee, especially whereas thou knowest all things that are among the Jews, customs and questions: for the which cause I beseech thee, hear me patiently. And my life truly from my youth, which was from the beginning in my nation in Jerusalem, all the Jews do know: knowing me before from the beginning (if they will give testimony) that according to the most sure sect of our religion I lived a Pharisee. And now for the hope of the promise that was made of God to our fathers, do I stand subject to judgement: the which, our twelve tribes serving night and day, hope to come unto. Of the which hope, o king, I am accused of the Jews. What incredible thing is it judged with you, if God raise the dead? And myself truly had thought that I ought to do against the name of JESUS of Nazareth many contrary things. Which also I did at Jerusalem, and many of the saints did I shut up in prisons, having received authority of the chief priests: and when they were put to death, I brought the sentence. And throughout all the synagogues oftentimes punishing them, I compelled them to blaspheme: and yet more mad against them, I persecuted them even unto foreign cities. Among which things whiles I went to Damascus with authority and permission of the chief priests, at mid-day, in the way, I saw (o king) from Heaven a light to have shined round about me and them that were in company with me, above the brightness of the sun. And when all we were fallen down on the ground, I heard a voice speaking to me in the Hebrew tongue: Saul, Saul, why persecutest thou me? It is hard for thee to kick against the prick. And I said:

Whereupon, king Agrippa, I was not incredulous to the heavenly vision: but to them first that are at Damascus, and at Jerusalem, and unto all the country of Jewry, and to the Gentiles did I preach that they should do penance, & turn to God, doing works worthy of penance. For this cause the Jews, when I was in the temple, apprehending me, attempted meaning to kill me. But aided by the help of God, I stand until this day, testifying to small & to great, saying nothing beside those things which the Prophets did speak should come to pass, & Moyses; if CHRIST were passible, if the first of the resurrection from the dead, he were to shew light to the people and to the Gentiles.
