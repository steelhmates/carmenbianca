Other seven also came up out of the river, foul, and carrion lean: and they fed on the very bank of the river, in green places: and they devoured them that had the marvelous beauty and good state of bodies. Pharao after he waked, slept again, and saw another dream: Seven ears of corn grew forth upon one stalk full and fair: there sprang also other ears as many, thin and blasted with adustion, devouring all the beauty of the former.
